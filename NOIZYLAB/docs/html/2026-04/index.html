Design Principles

Every product decision in NOIZYLAB runs through these five tests.

01

Reduce decoding friction

Large type. High contrast. Generous line height. Short chunks. White space is not wasted space — it is comprehension space.

02

Voice is the primary interface

Audio presentation of all core content. Human voice preferred over synthesis. Learners choose how they consume — read, listen, watch.

03

Context before content

Tell the learner where they are, what this is for, and why it matters — before delivering any information. Never skip orientation.

04

Emotion is data

Shame, curiosity, pride, resistance — these are not noise to be designed away. They are signals. The learning environment must be able to receive and respond to them.

05

Knowledge has a body

The people who know things are aging out. Recording their voice, their gesture, their way of explaining — that is preservation. UNESCO calls it living heritage. We call it the mission.

Platform Architecture

NOIZYLAB is not a single app. It is a layered system where each layer serves a different kind of learner need.

Layer 4
The DreamChamber
Voice-first AI interaction space. Learners bring questions, elders bring answers, AI facilitates the exchange.
Layer 3
Wisdom Archive
Structured repository of elder recordings, transcribed with consent, searchable by topic, trade, and tradition.
Layer 2
Learning Pathways
Modular, voice-annotated learning sequences designed around the dyslexic cognitive profile: big picture → context → detail.
Layer 1
Consent Kernel
Every voice, every recording, every piece of knowledge is gated by explicit elder consent. Revocable at any time. Non-negotiable.

Elder Transmission

"Intangible cultural heritage can only be safeguarded by the communities, groups and individuals who create, maintain and transmit it."
UNESCO Convention for the Safeguarding of Intangible Cultural Heritage, 2003

The elders are not a content library. They are living archives. Their knowledge does not exist in documents — it exists in their hands, their voices, their instincts built over decades. When they leave, that knowledge does not retire. It disappears.

NOIZYLAB's elder transmission model is built on three UNESCO-aligned pillars: intergenerational transfer, oral practice, and community-based education. These are not romantic ideas. They are the only mechanisms that have ever successfully moved complex tacit knowledge across generations.

🎙️

Record with Dignity

Elder-driven recording process. Their story, their pace, their terms.

🧬

Transmit, Don't Extract

Knowledge shared in relationship — not scraped, not stripped, not summarized without consent.

🔐

Consent is Sacred

Every recording is gated by a consent token. The elder revokes it, it's gone. Full stop.

🌱

Community Validates

Knowledge is reviewed by the community that holds it. Not by an algorithm. By people.

Version 1 — Launch Path

Six steps from here to a working pilot with real elders and real learners.

1

Consent infrastructure

Deploy the NOIZY consent kernel adapted for educational content. Every recording, every lesson gets a consent token before a single word is captured.

2

Elder onboarding protocol

A short, voice-explained intake process. No long forms. No legal jargon. A 15-minute conversation that establishes relationship, scope, and consent.

3

First 10 recordings

Pilot with 10 elder voices across 3 knowledge domains. Focus: craft, care, and community leadership. These become the seed archive.

4

Dyslexia-forward interface v1

A voice-first web interface that presents the recordings with full accessibility compliance, chunked transcripts, and emotional context headers.

5

First learner cohort

8–12 learners, recruited through neurodivergent learning communities. Paired directly with elders where possible. Documented for iteration.

6

Community review + v2 brief

Elder council reviews the pilot. Learner cohort reviews the pilot. What stays, what changes, what's missing — becomes the V2 brief.
