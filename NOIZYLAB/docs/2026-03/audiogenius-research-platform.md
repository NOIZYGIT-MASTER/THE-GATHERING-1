# AudioGenius Research Platform

## Summary

AudioGenius is an artist-led, neuroscience-informed sonic research platform.

It explores how structured sound environments may support nervous-system regulation and creative well-being without making unsupported medical cure claims.

## Safe Positioning

Use language like:

- neuroscience-informed auditory modulation
- structured sound research
- creative well-being support
- nervous-system engagement

Do not lead with:

- cures
- treatment replacement
- diagnostic capability

## One-Sentence Definition

AudioGenius is a consent-first platform for designing, studying, and experiencing structured sound environments that may support nervous-system engagement and creative well-being.

## Core Workstreams

### 1. Structured Sonic Design

Focus on:

- temporal modulation
- rhythmic entrainment
- spectral density
- dynamic range shaping
- spatialized audio cues

The goal is to study patterns, not market magic frequencies.

### 2. Neuroplasticity-Oriented Engagement

Explore repeated auditory exposure protocols for:

- attention
- calm
- focus
- sensory predictability
- subjective regulation

Frame outputs as exploratory and supportive, not therapeutic claims.

### 3. Brain And Biosignal Observation

Possible inputs:

- EEG
- heart rate variability
- galvanic skin response
- behavioral self-report

Use these to study correlations, not diagnose pathology.

### 4. Cross-Disciplinary R And D

AudioGenius should sit at the intersection of:

- neuroscience
- psychoacoustics
- creative sound design
- human-computer interaction
- artist-led experiential research

### 5. Adaptive Audio Algorithms

Allow responsive sound changes based on:

- self-declared state
- non-diagnostic biosignals
- tolerance history

Adaptation should remain user-guided and interpretable.

### 6. Education And Access

Support:

- workshops
- fellowships
- open protocols
- responsible creator tooling
- global access initiatives

## Evidence Ladder

### Tier 1

Well-established or clinically stronger adjacent domains:

- perioperative music support
- rhythmic auditory stimulation in neurorehab
- focused ultrasound as acoustic intervention

### Tier 2

Established but heterogeneous domains:

- tinnitus sound therapy
- neonatal music exposure research

### Tier 3

Emerging or exploratory domains:

- ASMR physiology studies
- vibroacoustic therapy
- ultrasound neuromodulation research

### Tier 4

Consumer-popular but uneven evidence:

- binaural beats
- generalized frequency-healing claims

## Ethical Guardrails

- no medical claims without trials
- no replacement-of-care claims
- informed consent for studies
- ethics review for structured research
- transparent data use
- identity-preserving data practices

## Relationship To NOIZY

AudioGenius is a research DreamChamber.

It lets artists, researchers, and facilitators study structured sound without turning participants into extractive datasets.
