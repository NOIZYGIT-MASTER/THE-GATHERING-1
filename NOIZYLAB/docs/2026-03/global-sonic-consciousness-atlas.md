## Container And Catalyst Rules

For NOIZY, the deep lesson is not "copy the sacred."
It is "respect the container."

The important recurring rules are:

- sound often organizes the experience
- guidance matters more than spectacle
- repetition can stabilize intensity
- communal witnessing changes interpretation
- entry and exit are part of the design, not extras
- catalysts are optional and never the product baseline

DreamChamber should learn from these rules while keeping:

- clear safety boundaries
- no fake clinical claims
- no flattened sacred presets
- visible cultural context
- room for sober, non-pharmacological depth

## Consciousness Levers

The most reusable cross-cultural levers are:

- pulse and repetition
- drone and sustained tone
- harmonic density
- breath pacing
- procession through space
- call and response
- silence and spaciousness
- reflection and closure

## Design Rules For NOIZY

- treat sound practices as living contexts, not costume pieces
- separate ceremonial inspiration from clinical or medical claims
- build container before intensity
- make communal modes explicit
- preserve provenance and cultural naming wherever possible
