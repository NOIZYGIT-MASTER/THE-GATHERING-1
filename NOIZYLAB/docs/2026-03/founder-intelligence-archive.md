## Thesis

NOIZY needs more than note storage.

It needs a founder-grade intelligence system that:

- captures ideas quickly
- promotes important concepts into canon
- preserves relationships between systems
- generates audience-specific narratives from one truth base
- stays usable by Claude and future collaborators

This is the difference between:

- a pile of ideas
- and a founder-level system

## Why It Matters

- the NOIZY vision is too large for chat history alone
- partner-facing opportunities require coherence, not raw volume
- product decisions need traceable evolution over time
- the archive itself becomes a strategic asset
- companies like Anthropic, Apple, and Google need system architecture, not inspirational fragments

## Current Model

The current archive is built on five layers:

1. capture
2. canon
3. graph
4. narrative
5. automation

The operating pattern is:

`capture -> distill -> link -> version -> brief -> refresh`

This creates stacked intelligence:

- raw capture
- durable canon
- semantic relationships
- audience-specific narratives
- automation and retrieval

The active operating layer for that stack is:

- [second-brain-ai.md](./second-brain-ai.md)

## Decisions

- decision: keep the archive local-first
  reason: ownership, portability, and speed matter more than SaaS convenience
  tradeoff: fewer out-of-the-box UI features

- decision: use many small canonical docs instead of giant master scrolls
  reason: AI tools reason better over modular source-of-truth files
  tradeoff: requires stronger indexing discipline

- decision: treat major ideas as versioned concepts
  reason: important systems will evolve, and the archive should show that evolution clearly
  tradeoff: adds a little process overhead
