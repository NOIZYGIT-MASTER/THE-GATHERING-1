# NOIZY 128 Scale Structures

## Summary

This is the NOIZY working taxonomy of 128 scale and mode structures distilled from recurring tonal behaviors across world cultures.

It is not a claim that all human pitch systems reduce to exactly 128 scientifically final categories.

It is a practical mapping layer for DreamChamber, the Global Music Genome, NOIZY School, and future machine-readable sonic registries.

Use it to:

- group related pitch worlds without forcing them into Western equivalence
- design playable tonal families for DreamChamber
- preserve differences between scale row, pathway, drone gravity, and modal behavior
- connect culture-specific systems to broader structural families

## Reading Rule

A structure here may describe:

- a pitch set
- a modal family
- a drone field
- a segment-chain system
- an intonation behavior
- a pathway-based melodic environment

It is a design taxonomy, not a replacement for lineage-specific naming.

## Public 8x16 Family Frame

For teaching, navigation, and top-level NOIZY product design, the 128 structures can also be presented as eight broad families of sixteen variations each:

1. Pentatonic Scales
2. Hexatonic Scales
3. Diatonic And Heptatonic Scales
4. Raga Structures
5. Maqam And Related Modal Structures
6. Gamelan And Southeast Asian Tuning Worlds
7. Chromatic And Western Extended Structures
8. Experimental, Microtonal, And Future Structures

This public frame is intentionally simpler than the deeper internal structure language below.

Use it when you need a high-level way to explain global pitch organization before dropping into segment chains, pathway logic, drone gravity, intonation fields, and hybrid synthetic systems.

### Public Family Notes

- **Pentatonic Scales:** major, minor, suspended, blues, East Asian, African, Celtic, Andean, and related five-tone worlds.
- **Hexatonic Scales:** six-tone omission fields, whole-tone variants, augmented fields, and hexatonic raga-like behaviors.
- **Diatonic And Heptatonic Scales:** church modes, tonal scales, harmonic and melodic variants, open seven-tone fields.
- **Raga Structures:** ascent/descent rules, omitted tones, crooked pathways, drone logic, time-of-day and devotional color.
- **Maqam And Related Modal Structures:** jins chains, seyir, microtonal interval color, cadential pathway systems across Arab, Anatolian, Persian-adjacent worlds.
- **Gamelan And Southeast Asian Tuning Worlds:** slendro, pelog, subset fields, gong-linked and metallophone-linked tonal systems.
- **Chromatic And Western Extended Structures:** chromatic, bebop, octatonic, quartal, quintal, and synthetic modern harmonic resources.
- **Experimental, Microtonal, And Future Structures:** quarter-tone, 19-tone, 31-tone, spectral, sensor-adaptive, biometric, and speculative pitch fields.

The detailed 128 structures below give DreamChamber a more implementation-ready tonal language.

## Family 01: Recitation And Narrow-Field Structures

- `S001` Single-Tone Axis: one pitch center with ornamental departure.
- `S002` Tone-Plus-Neighbor: reciting tone plus one active adjacent color.
- `S003` Lower-Neighbour Cell: center defined by downward pull.
- `S004` Upper-Neighbour Cell: center defined by upward reach.
- `S005` Minor-Third Cell: compressed tri-tone emotional nucleus.
- `S006` Major-Third Cell: brighter three-note nucleus around stable center.
- `S007` Neutral-Third Cell: flexible third space between major and minor color.
- `S008` Tritone Axis: tension field around a split center.
- `S009` Fourth-Shell: quartal perimeter defining melodic territory.
- `S010` Fifth-Drone Shell: tonic and dominant-style anchor only.
- `S011` Split-Tetrachord Seed: two linked small cells without full octave closure.
- `S012` Recitation-With-Cadence Tail: narrow field that opens only at phrase end.
- `S013` Lament Narrow Range: restrained contour with strong emotional inflection.
- `S014` Overtone Call Field: narrow pitch area shaped by harmonic resonance.
- `S015` Speech-Contour Field: pitch set follows verbal inflection more than interval plan.
- `S016` Threshold Invocation Cell: ceremonial opening structure with minimal pitch inventory.

## DreamChamber Translation Rule

DreamChamber should store each structure with:

- pitch inventory
- tonic or center behavior
- drone relation
- pathway behavior
- intonation notes
- ornament rules
- cadence tendencies
- lineage notes

The goal is not to trap the world inside 128 frozen scales.

The goal is to give the system a playable tonal language that can remain open to cultural specificity.
