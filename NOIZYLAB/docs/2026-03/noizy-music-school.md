# NOIZY Music School

## Summary

This is the music school many artists wished had existed for them.

Read [noizy-teaching-credo.md](./noizy-teaching-credo.md) for the short public-facing statement of belief.

It is not built around:

- gatekeeping
- standardization
- sterile theory drills
- passive spectatorship

It is built around:

- listening
- lineage
- embodiment
- co-creation
- sovereignty
- real artistic life support

## Core Thesis

The school should teach people how to become more humanly legible through art, not more mechanically correct.

It should help:

- self-taught creators
- world-building composers
- voice actors
- experimental producers
- disabled and adaptive artists
- culturally curious beginners
- kids entering through wonder instead of fear

## Why It Matters

Traditional music education often fragments the real thing.

It separates:

- body from theory
- culture from technique
- emotion from craft
- community from mastery
- art from livelihood

NOIZY Music School should reunite those layers.

## School Principles

### 1. Listening Before Performance

Students learn to hear:

- pulse
- space
- timbre
- breath
- silence
- emotional contour

before they are asked to optimize output.

### 2. Lineage Before Presets

Students do not browse flattened sound packs.

They enter:

- world biomes
- instrument families
- rhythm systems
- scale systems
- ritual functions
- cultural contexts

through DreamChamber and the Global Music Genome.

### 3. Body Before Abstraction

Rhythm starts in:

- breath
- gesture
- walking
- posture
- call and response

Nerve-to-Note and adaptive creation tools make this accessible to artists with different bodies and different limitations.

### 4. Participation Before Spectatorship

The school is built around:

- circles
- voice rooms
- rhythm rooms
- shared listening
- collaborative world-building
- first-take performance

Students learn by entering the field, not by staying outside it.

### 5. Direction As Resonance

Creative instruction is not only correction.

It is signal transmission.

AVA, NOIZYVOX, and the Creative Transmission Protocol make direction feel like invitation, not control.

### 6. Sovereignty Before Extraction

Students should understand:

- provenance
- consent
- attribution
- model rights
- artist-controlled monetization

from the beginning.

The school is not only about making art.
It is about protecting the source that makes it.

### 7. Life Support, Not One-Off Success

LIFELUV belongs in the school.

Artists should learn:

- how to build durable creative assets
- how to collaborate without surrendering rights
- how to turn taste, performance, teaching, and identity into recurring value

- experimentation
