# AUTISM-CALM: Calming Research for Autistic Individuals

**Research Code:** AUTISM-CALM
**Status:** Active Research
**Parent:** NOIZY.AI Research Division

---

## Mission

Develop and research auditory-based calming techniques specifically designed for autistic children and adults, with deep respect for neurodiversity and individual needs.

---

## Core Research Areas

### 1. Sound Sensitivity Understanding
- Hyperacusis and sound sensitivity patterns
- Auditory processing differences in autism
- Safe sound levels and frequency ranges
- Individual variation in sound preferences

### 2. Calming Sound Design
- Non-intrusive audio environments
- Predictable sound patterns (reducing anxiety)
- Nature sounds and their effects
- White/pink/brown noise applications

### 3. Sensory Regulation
- Sound as a tool for self-regulation
- Transition support (audio cues)
- Meltdown prevention through audio
- Stim-friendly sound experiences

### 4. Personalization
- Individual sound preference mapping
- Customizable audio environments
- User-controlled sound parameters
- Preference learning systems

---

## Key Considerations

### Ethical Framework
- **Acceptance, not cure** - Supporting comfort, not "fixing"
- **Individual agency** - User controls their experience
- **Sensory autonomy** - Right to modify environment
- **Neurodiversity respect** - Different is not deficit

### Safety Guidelines
- Avoid sudden sounds
- Predictable audio patterns
- Volume control always accessible
- Easy exit/stop mechanisms
- No forced exposure

---

## Research Questions

1. What sound characteristics are most commonly calming for autistic individuals?
2. How does sound sensitivity vary across the autism spectrum?
3. Can personalized sound profiles reduce anxiety/distress?
4. What role does predictability play in sound comfort?
5. How can technology adapt to individual preferences?
