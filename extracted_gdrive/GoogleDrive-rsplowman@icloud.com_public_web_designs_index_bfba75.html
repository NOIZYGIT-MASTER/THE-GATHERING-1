Vintage Tape

Overview

Vintage Tape, inspired by the Studer A810 two-track tape deck, allows you to customize the frequency response and saturation characteristics of magnetic tape without the crosstalk, hiss, wow, and flutter that could potentially ruin your mix.

Module Interface

Ozone 9 Vintage Tape module controls and features are outlined in the table below.

Reset

Returns all controls in the Vintage Tape module to their factory default values.

UNDO HISTORY TO REVERT SETTINGS

Controls

The Control Panel for the Vintage Tape Module includes:

  • Speed
  • Input Drive
  • Bias
  • Harmonics
  • Low Emphasis
  • High Emphasis

Speed

Select from one of three standard tape speeds in inches per second (IPS) to tailor the frequency response and levels at which frequencies experience distortion.

  • High frequencies (small wavelengths) distort at lower input levels than low frequencies (long wavelengths).
  • Changing the speed changes the wavelength (wavelength=speed/frequency). For example, a 500Hz signal at 7.5 ips will experience distortion at the same level as a 1kHz signal at 15 ips and a 2kHz signal at 30 ips.
SPEED EFFECT
7.5 Adds warmth by emphasizing the signal’s low end.
15 Adds a distinct body and thickness to your track.
30 Adds a subtle, refined polish with higher-fidelity and lower-noise.

Input Drive

Adjusts input gain before tape emulation. The harder you drive the input, the more compression and saturation will occur.

INPUT DRIVE EFFECT
HIGHER INPUT DRIVE LEVEL Can lead to more saturation in tape emulation.
LOWER INPUT DRIVE LEVEL Can reduce added saturation in tape emulation.

Saturation and Tape

Tape becomes non-linear when recording high amplitude signals, leading to saturation. The level necessary to achieve a certain amount of saturation depends on the frequency content of the source material and the bias setting.

Bias

Adjust to customize the shape of the distortion curve and high-frequency response of the tape emulation. Both negative and positive bias will add distortion in different ways as described in the table below.

BIAS EFFECT
NEGATIVE BIAS Boosts at the higher frequencies similar to an under-biased tape machine.
POSITIVE BIAS Attenuates some of the higher frequencies and at high values can severely reduce dynamic-range providing more compression similar to an over-biased tape machine.

Extreme Bias for Extreme Effect

If you want to add some serious crunch to stems and mixes, move the bias control towards its extremes and conjure the vibe of an old workhorse tape machine.

Vintage Tape Calculates Your Output Levels

On a real tape machine, any adjustments made to bias, speed, or input drive requires recalibration to all the other variables. The Vintage Tape module intelligently calculates these shifts while you make adjustments.

Harmonics

Adjust the slider to add even-harmonic distortion. This saturation can be used to add complexity and richness.

HARMONICS EFFECT
0 (DEFAULT) Tape emulation similar to the odd-harmonic saturation of magnetic tape.
ABOVE ZERO Tape emulation similar to even-harmonic saturation introduced by inaccuracies in the AC bias design or distortion from machine electronics.

Low Emphasis

Controls the shape of the resonant peak (also known as “Head Bump”) in dB, which adds gain or a gentle peak to bass or kick drum material. Keeping this control at 2.0 will add a very subtle amount of head bump, but pushing the higher limits of this slider can give you low-end power.

LOW EMPHASIS VALUE (dB) EFFECT
LOWER VALUE Removes the resonant peak, flattening out the frequency response while maintaining the low-end rolloff. This can help achieve the warmth of analog without adding mud or too much bass.
HIGHER VALUE Boosts the resonant peak up to 10dB, adding low end punch and emphasis.

Tape Speed Affects Resonant Frequency

Similar to a traditional tape machine, the resonant frequency is affected by the tape speed you select.

  • At 30 IPS, the head bump sits right around 50 Hz.
  • At 15 IPS, the head bump sits an octave below that.

High Emphasis

Adjust to compensate for high-frequency losses to give your audio extra energy without being too harsh or bright. The default setting of 4.0 gives you an authentic, close-to-flat high frequency response.

HIGH EMPHASIS VALUE (dB) EFFECT
LOWER VALUE Adds gentle, roll-off at high-frequencies.
HIGHER VALUE Adds a high-end shimmer.
