Getting Started

Overview

Welcome to Tonal Balance Control 2 (TBC 2)! If you have never used an iZotope product or want to know more about TBC, then this is the right chapter for you. TBC2 currently supports:

  • Ozone 9.0 (EQ)
  • Neutron 3.1 (EQ)
  • Nectar 3.1 (EQ)
  • Relay 1.03

The topics discussed in this chapter include:

  • Authorization
  • Navigating the Interface
  • Working with IPC
  • Workflow Suggestions

Authorization

The first time you open an Ozone 9 or Neutron 3.1 plug-in, the Authorization window will appear allowing you to also authorize Tonal Balance Control 2

The Authorization window allows you to:

  • TRIAL: Start or continue a Trial period evaluation prior to purchasing.
  • DEMO: Continue evaluating the product with Demo limitations (after the 10 day Trial period ends).
  • AUTHORIZE: Authorize the product with a serial number.

Trial Mode

Trial mode allows you to evaluate TBC over a 10 day trial period. The trial period begins when you first open a TBC plug-in in a DAW/NLE. The Authorization window will display the number of days remaining in your trial period. Click the Continue button to exit the Authorization window.

Demo Mode

After your 10 day trial period expires, you have the option to operate TBC in Demo mode. To continue evaluating TBC in demo mode, click the Demo button.

Authorization Methods

To disable Trial or Demo mode, you must authorize the product with a valid serial number. We offer three authorization methods for TBC:

  1. Online Authorization: Authorize on a computer online.
  2. Offline Authorization: Authorize on a computer offline.
  3. iLok Authorization: Authorize using iLok.

INFO: More Authorization Help

  • For information about Authorization, please visit the iZotope website: https://www.izotope.com/authorization
  • For additional help authorizing TBC, visit iZotope Customer Care information on the iZotope website: http://www.izotope.com/support or contact our Customer Care department by sending an email to: support@izotope.com.
  • More information about iZotope’s Customer Care department and policies can be found in the iZotope Customer Care chapter.

Working With IPC

Tonal Balance Control connects to other iZotope plug-ins via Inter Plug-in Communication (IPC) technology allowing you to see the EQ spectrums from Neutron, Ozone, Nectar, and Relay. TBC 2 will only display interactive EQ information from Ozone 9, Neutron 3.1, and Nectar 3.1. For more information on IPC sources, see the IPC Sources chapter.

TBC 2 is not Backwards compatible

Due to the new functionality Smooth Metering, Tonal Balance Control 2 can NOT work with any plug-ins before the following iZotope plug-in versions:

  • Ozone 9.0
  • Neutron 3.1
  • Nectar 3.1
  • Relay 1.03

Workflow Suggestions

To get the most out of the Tonal Balance Control plug-in, consider the following suggested workflow.

TBC is a meter that can be used to guide your decisions. If part of the spectrum falls outside of the recommended bounds, you can do the following:

  • Change the level of instruments or group of instruments (stems) that are causing the spectrum to be out of bounds.
  • To equalize individual instruments or stems that may be causing the spectrum to be out of bounds.

To use TBC to the fullest of its intended effect:

  1. TBC 2 should always be placed last on the master bus.
  2. TBC 2 is intended to be used on a full mix and may not yield expected results on individual tracks in a session.

Importance of Placing TBC in Your Processing Chain

We highly recommend that TBC is inserted at the end of the processing chain on your master bus track. This ensures that the target curve you are comparing to is after all the other processing in your session.
