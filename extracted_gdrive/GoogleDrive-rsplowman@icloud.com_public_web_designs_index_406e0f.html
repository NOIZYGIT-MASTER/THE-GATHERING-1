Vintage EQ

Overview

The Vintage EQ module can be used to add the emulated frequency response of the Pultec EQP-1A and Pultec MEQ-5.

Module Interface

The Ozone 9 Vintage EQ module controls and features are outlined in the table below.

Channel Processing Modes

The Vintage EQ module can operate in either Stereo mode or Mid/Side mode. See the General Controls chapter for more information regarding Mid/Side Processing.

Reset

Returns all controls in the Vintage EQ module to their factory default values.

UNDO HISTORY TO REVERT SETTINGS

If you wish to return to the settings you were using before clicking the Reset button, you can use the Undo History window to revert the settings.

Controls

The Vintage EQ control panel includes two sets of controls: the Pultec EQP-1A Equalizer and the Pultec MEQ-5 Equalizer.

The top row of parameters are modeled after the Pultec EQP-1A Equalizer. This includes the following controls which are further described in the tables below:

  • Low Frequency
  • High Frequency Boost
  • High Frequency Cut

The bottom row of parameters are modeled after the Pultec MEQ-5 Equalizer. This includes the following controls which are further described in the tables below:

  • Mid Frequency
  • Low-Mid Frequency
  • High-Mid Frequency

Pultec EQP-1A

A unique feature of the Pultec EQP-1A is the ability to simultaneously adjust the boost and cut parameters for the low frequency band. While the original manual warns “Do not attempt to boost and attenuate simultaneously,” these controls interact in a way that many engineers have found to be desirable. Rather than canceling each other out, combining these controls results in a boost in the low frequencies, followed by a dip at slightly higher frequencies. This unique filter shape is also available in the Ozone Equalizer module as “Vintage Low Shelf”.

Low Frequency

CONTROL DESCRIPTION
LOW FREQUENCY Sets the low frequency to be boosted. Options include: 20 Hz, 30 Hz, 45 Hz, 60 Hz, and 100 Hz.
LOW BOOST Sets the amount of boost applied to the selected low frequency. This is a low shelf filter.
LOW CUT Sets the amount of attenuation applied to the selected low frequency. This is a low shelf filter.

Low Frequency Selection Note

  • The 45 Hz option isn’t present in the original hardware unit, but we’ve added this frequency because we found it to be useful for mastering.

High Frequency Boost

CONTROL DESCRIPTION
HIGH BOOST FREQUENCY Sets the high frequency to be boosted. Options include 3 kHz, 4 kHz, 5 kHz, 8 kHz, 10 kHz, 12 kHz, and 16 kHz.
HIGH BOOST Sets the amount of boost applied to the selected high frequency. This is a peaking filter.
HIGH Q Sets the bandwidth for high frequency boosting. The boost amount and bandwidth interact (i.e., the Q changes as you boost), as they do in the EQP-1A.

High Frequency Cut Controls

CONTROL DESCRIPTION
HIGH CUT FREQUENCY Sets the high frequency to be attenuated. Options include 5 kHz, 10 kHz, and 20kHz.
HIGH CUT Sets the amount of attenuation applied to the selected high frequency. This is a high shelf filter.

Pultec MEQ-5 Equalizer Controls

All of the mid frequency controls are peaking filters. As in the original MEQ-5, the filter bandwidths are affected by the boost/cut amount.

Low-Mid Frequency Controls

CONTROL DESCRIPTION
LOW-MID BOOST FREQUENCY Sets the low-mid frequency to be boosted. Options include 200 Hz, 300 Hz, 500 Hz, 700 Hz, and 1000 Hz.
LM BOOST FREQUENCY Sets the amount of boost applied to the selected low-mid frequency band.

Mid Frequency Cut Controls

CONTROL DESCRIPTION
MID CUT FREQUENCY Sets the mid frequency to be attenuated. Options include 200 Hz, 300 Hz, 500 Hz, 700 Hz, 1 kHz, 1.5 kHz, 2 kHz, 3 kHz, 4 kHz, 5 kHz, and 7 kHz.
MID CUT Sets the amount of attenuation applied to the selected mid frequency.

High-Mid Frequency Controls

CONTROL DESCRIPTION
HIGH-MID BOOST FREQUENCY Sets the high-mid frequency to be boosted. Options include 1.5 kHz, 2 kHz, 3 kHz, 4 kHz, and 5 kHz.
HM BOOST Sets the amount of boost applied to the selected high-mid frequency.
