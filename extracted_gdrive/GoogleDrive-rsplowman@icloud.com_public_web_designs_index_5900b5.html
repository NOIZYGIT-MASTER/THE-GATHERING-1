Tonal Balance Control Help Documentation

TBC 2 Help Documentation

Introduction

Getting your mix or master to sit just right across the frequency spectrum is a difficult task. Tonal Balance Control was created to make this process more achievable for everyone.

Tonal Balance Control 2 is included with Ozone 9 Advanced and Neutron 3.1 Advanced.

Getting Started

Overview

Welcome to Tonal Balance Control 2 (TBC 2)! If you have never used an iZotope product or want to know more about TBC, then this is the right chapter for you. TBC2 currently supports:

  • Ozone 9.0 (EQ)
  • Neutron 3.1 (EQ)
  • Nectar 3.1 (EQ)
  • Relay 1.03

Authorization

The first time you open an Ozone 9 or Neutron 3.1 plug-in, the Authorization window will appear allowing you to also authorize Tonal Balance Control 2

The Authorization window allows you to:

  • TRIAL: Start or continue a Trial period evaluation prior to purchasing.
  • DEMO: Continue evaluating the product with Demo limitations (after the 10 day Trial period ends).
  • AUTHORIZE: Authorize the product with a serial number.

Trial Mode

Trial mode allows you to evaluate TBC over a 10 day trial period. The trial period begins when you first open a TBC plug-in in a DAW/NLE. The Authorization window will display the number of days remaining in your trial period. Click the Continue button to exit the Authorization window.

Demo Mode

After your 10 day trial period expires, you have the option to operate TBC in Demo mode. To continue evaluating TBC in demo mode, click the Demo button.

Authorization Methods

To disable Trial or Demo mode, you must authorize the product with a valid serial number. We offer three authorization methods for TBC:

  1. Online Authorization: Authorize on a computer online.
  2. Offline Authorization: Authorize on a computer offline.
  3. iLok Authorization: Authorize using iLok.

Working With IPC

Tonal Balance Control connects to other iZotope plug-ins via Inter Plug-in Communication (IPC) technology allowing you to see the EQ spectrums from Neutron, Ozone, Nectar, and Relay. TBC 2 will only display interactive EQ information from Ozone 9, Neutron 3.1, and Nectar 3.1. For more information on IPC sources, see the IPC Sources chapter.

TBC 2 is not Backwards compatible

Due to the new functionality Smooth Metering, Tonal Balance Control 2 can NOT work with any plug-ins before the following iZotope plug-in versions:

  • Ozone 9.0
  • Neutron 3.1
  • Nectar 3.1
  • Relay 1.03

Workflow Suggestions

To get the most out of the Tonal Balance Control plug-in, consider the following suggested workflow.

TBC is a meter that can be used to guide your decisions. If part of the spectrum falls outside of the recommended bounds, you can do the following:

  • Change the level of instruments or group of instruments (stems) that are causing the spectrum to be out of bounds.
  • To equalize individual instruments or stems that may be causing the spectrum to be out of bounds.

To use TBC to the fullest of its intended effect:

  1. TBC 2 should always be placed last on the master bus.
  2. TBC 2 is intended to be used on a full mix and may not yield expected results on individual tracks in a session.

Importance of Placing TBC in Your Processing Chain

We highly recommend that TBC is inserted at the end of the processing chain on your master bus track. This ensures that the target curve you are comparing to is after all the other processing in your session.

Targets and Views

Overview

Tonal Balance Control displays spectrum metering information relative to a target curve for the track it is currently inserted on. This is particularly useful when dealing with tonal balance issues that arise during mixing and mastering. Tonal Balance Control can help provide context and direction toward resolving issues with a mix or master lacking the low-end power or high-end brilliance found in professionally produced, radio-ready material.

Based on the interface layout, you can access controls for two different views:

  • Target Curve View
  • IPC Plug-in View

Target Curve View

Displays incoming signal information over a spectrum display. Use the Meters View Selector, Meter View Controls and Indicators, and Target Curve Selection to tailor your Target Curve View experience.

Meter View Selector

There are two meter views that display signal information over a spectrum available in TBC 2 for you to monitor:

  • Broad View
  • Fine View

Broad View

Displays four target curve ranges based on the following frequency ranges outlined in the table below.

  • The blue bars shown in each frequency band represent the typical range of spectral variation for the selected target curve.
  • The white lines will update to show how your track compares to the target curve.
BROAD RANGE FREQUENCY RANGE
Low 20 Hz - 250 Hz
Low-mid 250 Hz - 2 kHz
High-mid 2 kHz - 8 kHz
High 8 kHz - 20 kHz

Modify EQ Instances To Meet Target Ranges

You can make adjustments to Ozone, Neutron, and Nectar EQ instances to modify your mix to fall within the blue target ranges.

Fine View

Displays the selected target curve range in blue across the frequency spectrum. The white spectrum curve in this view represents your current track.

Meter View Controls and Indicators

The following Meter View controls are accessible in both Broad View and Fine View:

LABEL CONTROL/ INDICATOR DESCRIPTION
Crest Factor Displays information about the low end dynamic range of your track. When the meter is weighted to the left, it indicates that your mix is too dynamic in the low end. When weighted to the right, it indicates that your mix is too compressed in the low end.
Solo Click and hold to listen to a specfic frequency range of your audio.
Band Indicators Displays the frequency band labels in white when your current track is within the typical range for the selected target curve.

How to Use the Crest Factor Meter in TBC

Crest Factor is the ratio of the peak level of the RMS (average) level resulting in the average loudness. This is an important meter when dealing with low-frequency transients and instruments such as kick drum and bass guitar (not string or bluegrass) since dynamic range of the low end is crucial in a good master.

The meter indicates two extremes:

  • More Dynamic (Left-bound): large dynamic range (eg. your kick drum is louder).
  • More Compressed (Right-bound): minimal dynamic range.

You can interpret the guiding bounds in the following ways:

  • If within bounds: your low-end loudness is measured as common.
  • If out of bounds: measurement allows you to think about your musical decisions related to your ultimate goals for dynamic range in a track.

Target Curve Selection

You can either select a genre-based factory Target Curve or create a custom Target Curve to display over the meter view.

Factory Target Curve

Select from 12 preset target curves within the Target Curve selection menu to display over the meter view. A typical spectral curve found in major musical styles including: Bass Heavy, Country, EDM, Folk, Hip Hop, Jazz, Modern, Orchestral, Pop, Reggae, RnB-Soul, Rock.

More Information on Preset Target Curves

The factory target curves in Tonal Balance Control represent typical spectral shapes found in different styles of music. These target curves were created by analyzing the spectral characteristics of numerous tracks across different genres/styles of music. The target curves display ranges of typical spectral variation for these similar styles of music.

You can remotely impact an iZotope IPC plug-in’s EQ curve through TBC. This can be helpful when comparing different spectrums between your plug-ins since you can adjust several EQ curves in real-time and in one view.

There are two sets of controls in the EQ module:

  • EQ Controls
  • Band Controls

EQ controls are available with the EQ-related modules and plug-in as outlined in the table below.

Adjusting EQ Band Parameters

Unlike our other EQ plug-ins, in TBC a HUD will not appear as soon as a node is selected. Instead, whenever a node is selected, any adjustments to the available band parameters can be see in the EQ controls bar. You can adjust the EQ band parameters by interacting with the nodes using the adjustments described in the table and image below.

PARAMETER ADJUSTMENTS
Frequency
  • Click and drag EQ node left to decrease the frequency or right to increase the frequency.
  • Manually enter the value as described below.
  • Use LEFT and RIGHT arrow keys.
Gain
  • Click and drag EQ node down to decrease the gain or up to increase the gain.
  • Manually enter the value as described below.
  • Use UP and DOWN arrow keys.
Q (BANDWIDTH)
  • Click and drag EQ node handles toward node to narrow the bandwidth or away from the node to widen the bandwidth.
  • Manually enter the value as described below.
  • Use alt/ option + arrow keys.
Threshold Manually enter the value as described below.

To manually enter the value for Frequency, Gain, Q, or Threshold:

  1. Double click on the value readout of the metric you wish to change.
  2. Type in the value desired in the text edit field.
  3. Hit enter or return to save the value adjustment.

TIP: Keyboard Modifiers For Value Adjustments

  • Hold the Shift key while clicking and dragging the node to lock the movement to the horizontal axis (for frequency) and vertical axis (for gain).
  • Hold the Shift key while using the arrow keys to make coarse value adjustments.
  • Hold the command key (Mac) or ctrl key (Windows) while using the arrow keys to make fine value adjustments

NOTE: Reset All Band Parameters

You can reset all parameters for a particular band to their default values by double clicking on the node.

NOTE: EQ Gain Scale

Use the EQ Gain scale, located on the right edge of the EQ module display to reference dB magnitude while adjusting nodes.

IPC Sources With EQ Compatability

The EQ controls and readouts available in the EQ controls bar are related to the EQ HUDs from the following source plug-ins: Ozone 9, Neutron 3.1, Nectar 3.1.

EQ Curve View vs. Spectrum View

The type of view displayed depends on if the source is an Equalizer component plug-in or includes an Equalizer module in the mothership plug-in. For more information on the difference between the Spectrum view and the EQ curve view, see the IPC Plug-in View section.

Controls

The following controls are available for Ozone 9, Neutron 3.1, Nectar 3.1 EQ Curve View compatible instances:

Label Control Description
Power Button Enable or disable a selected EQ band.
Remove Removes a band node from the display and from the source plug-in EQ in real-time when a node is selected.
Filter Shape Menu Select filter shape to apply to a selected band node. The filter shapes that are available in this menu come from the respective source plug-in.
Dynamic Mode Enable to apply dynamic variable gain to the selected node in relation to the threshold setting. Boosting or attenuating is dependent on the UP/DOWN control and is triggered by the signal crossing the threshold.
UP/DOWN Select to either boost (UP) or to attenuate (DOWN) the signal in relation to the band node once the signal crosses the threshold.

Dynamic Mode Indicators

When Dynamic mode is enabled for an EQ band, directional arrows will be displayed above and below the applicable node in the EQ curve display. The arrow icon direction reflects the selected Dynamic Trigger Direction. You can also change the direction by clicking on these direction indicators.

Readouts

The following readouts are only available for Neutron 3.1 and Nectar 3.1 EQ Curve View compatible instances:

Readout Units Description
Frequency Hz Shows the center frequency (or cutoff frequency) of the currently selected node. The EQ supports frequency values ranging from 20 Hz to 20 kHz.
Gain dB Shows the amount of gain applied by the selected filter. The EQ supports gain adjustments ranging from -30 dB to +15 dB.
Q (cF/Bandwidth) (Parametric/Bell Filters) Shows the width or slope of the selected filter.
Threshold dB (Dynamic Mode enabled) Defines the point at which attenuation or boosting can occur.

Adjusting EQ Band Parameters

For more information on how to adjust the readouts, see the Adjusting EQ Band Parameters section in the Targets and Views chapter.
