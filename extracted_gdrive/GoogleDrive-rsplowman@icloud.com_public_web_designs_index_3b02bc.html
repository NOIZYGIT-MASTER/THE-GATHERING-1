Dither

Overview

Ozone contains a comprehensive set of dithering tools, bit meters, and DC offset meters to help you prepare studio-quality audio for CD and other formats, by effectively converting and dithering audio to 24, 20, 16, 12, or 8 bit using iZotope’s MBIT+ technology for exceptionally transparent conversion to different bit depths.

Dither does not include Maximizer

Dither processing is independent of the Maximizer module.

Accessing Dither

Access Dither by clicking on the Dither button below the I/O meters. Enable or disable Dither by clicking Dither’s power button.

Noise Shaping Curve View

Displays the noise-shaping profile curve across a frequency spectrum. The drawn curve represents the general frequency curve of the specific dither noise profile.

Controls

Dither includes the following set of controls in the control panel which allow you to talior your audio conversion processing:

  • Bit Depth
  • Dither Amount
  • Noise Shaping
  • Bit Meter
  • DC Offset Meter

Bit Depth

Select the target bit depth for your audio: 24, 20, 16,12, or 8.

Ideal CD Bit Options

When mastering for CD, industry standards recommend your Bit Depth be set to 16 bit.

Note: 8 & 12 Bit Options

The 8 & 12 bit options in the Dither panel are preview only WITHIN the Ozone application, you cannot use 8 or 12 bit Dither when exporting from the Ozone application.

Auto-Blanking

Selecting this option will completely mute dither output (i.e. dither noise) when the input signal is completely silent (0 bits of audio) for at least 0.7 seconds.

Dither Amount

Select the target number of bits or amount of dither that will be used. Dither Amount options are outlined in the table below

DITHER AMOUNT EFFECT
Strong Completely eliminates the non-linear distortion issues that may result from using Off or Low, but this comes at the expense of a slightly increased noise floor.
Medium Recommended choice for the Dither Amount setting.
Low Can leave some non-linear quantization distortion or dither noise modulation.
Off Can leave some non-linear quantization distortion or dither noise modulation.

Harmonic Suppression

Enable to slightly alter the truncation rules, moving the harmonic quantization distortion away from overtones of audible frequencies to maintain better tonal quality in the resulting signal.

Harmonic Suppresion and Artifacts

This option doesn’t create any random dithering noise floor. Truncation results in harmonic quantization distortion that adds overtones to the signal and distorts the timbre.

Limit Peaks

When enabled, the Limit Peaks control allows you to effectively suppress the spurious peaks caused by aggressive dither settings.

Dither Noise and Spurious Peaks

Dither noise is random in nature and has a very low amplitude, so dither peaks are not often a problem. However, depending on your dithering settings, especially more aggressive modes, the high frequency dither noise can be amplified significantly after noise shaping. This can result in spurious peaks in the overall dither signal (up to -60 dBFS for 16-bit quantization).

Noise Shaping

Selects the amount of noise shaping that is applied during the dithering process ranging from Off (no shaping) through Max (roughly 14 dB of audible noise suppression).

Noise Shaping Effects

Noise shaping pushes the noise necessary for dithering into less audible frequency ranges, allowing for greater dithering with less perceived noise. Different amounts of noise shaping will have subtle effects on the sound quality of the dither process, so experiment with the different settings with your particular program material.

Bit Meter

Monitor the digital activity of your program material and the state of bits to look for problems or discontinuities in the digital signal. This can be an invaluable resource for monitoring the digital activity of your program material, including viewing:

  • Range of bits (dynamic range) being used
  • Output word length
  • Faulty A/D converters
  • Sub-par plug-in processing

Bit Meter Columns

The columns bit meter provides specific information on the activity of the bits comprising your audio. The columns show which bits are being used. If a bit is used (goes from 1 to 0 or vice versa), the position for that bit is lit.The image and table blow outline the type of activiy being displayed by each column pair.

LABEL COLUMN REFERENCE DESCRIPTION
Inner Columns Displays the real-time activity of the bits of audio, for the left and right channels.
Outer Columns Displays the real-time activity in a “peak hold” way, retaining momentary information. If the inner columns toggle or “light up”, the outer columns will show that bit as being used.

Using the Bit Meter Effectively

In general, you want to see activity on each of the bits (except the top one, see below for explanation). Meaning that over a period of a few seconds each of the bits in the outside columns should be lit.

You should also click on the meter periodically to reset the peak hold of the outside columns. Something like DC offset would toggle a lower bit once (lighting the outside column), but would never toggle it after that since the bit is being held.

If you’re dithering down, you only want to see 16, 12, or 8 bits lit (corresponding to the output bit depth of the dither).

Identifying Signal Problems in Bit Meter

Some examples of problems you can identify with a bit meter are:

  • HEADROOM: You may have a 24 bit audio file, but aren’t using all of the bits.
  • 16 BITS: In a more extreme case, you are only using 16 bits. If you’re dithering to 16 bits, this is what you want to see. However, if you’re not dithering, this would suggest there is a problem somewhere, either an A/D converter that is only putting out 16 bits, a plug-in that’s truncating the signal down to 16 bits, or others.
  • STUCK BIT: One bit in the middle of the signal isn’t being exercised, possibly due to a faulty A/D converter or bug with a software plug-in.

Why the Top Bit Will Never Light Up in the Bit Meter

  • When samples are stored as a binary number, negative samples are specially encoded. Since the binary representation of negative numbers is not intuitive, Ozone takes the absolute value of each sample before plotting it on the bit meter.
  • Since the most significant bit is only set when a sample is negative, this bit will never light up. We simply included the top bit as placeholder for completeness.

Reset

Enable to reset the bit meter and start its display over with the program material’s current playback point.

DC Offset Meter

Displays the amount of the DC that is being removed when Filter is enabled.

Filter

Enable to activates a high-pass filter with a 1 Hz cutoff frequency that filters DC Offset before limiting.

DC Offset Requires Maximizer or Vintage Limiter

You cannot access the DC offset meter or Filter control unless you have Maximizer or Vintage-Limiter in your signal chain.

More information about DC Offset

DC offset is a defect in the recording when the waveform’s average voltage is above or below zero. Audio signals are normally represented by an alternating current (AC) whose average value is zero. Some malfunctions in the recording equipment can result in a direct current leaking into the signal. Thus, the recorded waveform appears to be above or below the zero line.

In FFT (Fast Fourier Transform) analyzers, the DC offset is represented with energy at zero frequency (0 Hz). A DC offset can be removed with a high-pass filter. It is important to distinguish DC offset from waveform asymmetry. Asymmetry skews the waveform shape towards positive or negative levels, but the average current stays at zero. Unlike DC offset, asymmetry has no particular energy near 0 Hz and can be reduced using phase rotation filters.

Dither in Ozone 9 Component Plug-ins

When Using the Plug-In Version of Ozone 9:

  • Do NOT perform any processing to your audio AFTER applying Dither. It is OK, however, to perform level adjustment with the Output Gain sliders in Ozone’s I/O panel, but do NOT change any levels in the host app or with other plug-ins.
  • Almost all host apps have their master faders after the effects slot, so any level adjustment in the host app will destroy the dither.
  • Do not put any plug-ins after Ozone if you are dithering with Ozone. The dither must be the last thing that touches the audio.
  • Turn off dithering in the host app.
