L2
Consent
NOIZY Consent Protocol (NCP)
Every use of an HVS voice requires a Consent Transaction Record (CTR) — a signed, timestamped, scoped permission specifying: who, what, how long, which uses, which territories, revocation terms. No CTR = no generation. No CTR = no licensing. No CTR = no training. NCP is published as an open protocol. Any platform integrating HVS data must implement NCP at the API level. Consent cannot be proxied, assigned, or inherited by a label. Only the artist signs.

Constitutional law

The Never Clauses.
Immutable. Non-negotiable.

These are not configuration options. They are not default settings. If any of these clauses change, the entire consent ledger must recompile its governance hashes. This is how trust is maintained.

NC-001
No voice export without a signed Consent Transaction Record
A voice cannot leave the HVS system without explicit, written, scoped consent from the artist. Not implied. Not legacy. Not label-assigned. Artist-signed.
NC-002
No AI training outside the approved use scope
Consent for one use does not imply consent for training. Game NPC consent does not include model training. Every scope is explicit. Every scope is bounded.
NC-003
No identity impersonation — living or deceased
A licensed voice cannot be used to impersonate the artist in contexts they did not consent to. The voice and the person are not separable assets.
NC-004
No sublicensing without artist ratification
A licensee cannot grant another party access to the voice. Every downstream use requires a new CTR. No cascade licensing. No label proxying.
NC-005
No perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free license — ever
The clause that burned ElevenLabs in 2025 cannot exist in HVS. Maximum license duration is 10 years per the NO FAKES Act. All licenses are revocable.
NC-006
No deployment in political advertising, medical advice, or non-consensual intimate content
Three categories are unconditionally prohibited regardless of consent offered. These protect the artist from uses that could harm their reputation, health, or dignity.
