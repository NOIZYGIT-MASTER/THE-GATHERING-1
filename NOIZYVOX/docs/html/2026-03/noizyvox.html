01 Identity & Fingerprint Layer SHA-256

Every voice recording submitted to the vault is hashed using SHA-256 on entry. This fingerprint is the cryptographic identity of the creator's vocal contribution. It cannot be altered, removed, or forged.

  • SHA-256 fingerprint generated at upload time
  • Fingerprint bound to creator's legal identity record
  • Stored immutably in the NOIZYVOX vault
  • Referenced in every downstream license event
02 Consent Key Layer Cryptographic

The consent key is generated when the creator signs the Seven Never Clauses during onboarding. It is a live, session-scoped cryptographic token that gates every license request. A request without a valid key does not proceed.

  • Generated at Seven Never Clauses signing
  • Bound to creator's vault record
  • Required for every license issuance
  • Cannot be delegated or transferred
03 Context Rule Layer Hard Constraints

Context rules are the creator's explicit permission set. Permitted contexts and blocked contexts are stored in the vault and checked against every license request before any output is generated. Blocks are hard walls — not soft preferences.

  • Permitted contexts: narration, drama, documentary, calm stack, etc.
  • Blocked contexts: political, military, adult content, etc.
  • Context check is mandatory — no bypass path exists
  • Creator can update context rules at any time; changes apply forward only
04 Royalty Ledger Layer Immutable

Every validated license event is written to the creator's royalty ledger before output is released. The ledger is append-only. Entries cannot be deleted or altered. The 75/25 split is calculated and logged at the moment of each event.

  • 75% to creator · 25% to platform — after Day 30
  • 50/50 during 30-day activation window
  • Ledger entries are timestamped and source-attributed
  • CSV export available at any time
05 Generational Transfer Layer Legacy Protocol

The voice vault is a life asset. Guild members can designate beneficiaries who inherit the vault, the royalty stream, and the consent key governance. The transfer protocol ensures continuity of the creator's context rules and attribution identity.

  • Beneficiary designation available from Day 1
  • Transfer triggers on creator-designated conditions
  • Inheriting party assumes full consent governance
  • Royalty history is preserved and transferred intact
