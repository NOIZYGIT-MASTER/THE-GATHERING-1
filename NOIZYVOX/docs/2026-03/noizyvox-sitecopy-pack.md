### Hero

Headline:

`A calmer listening space for sensitive nervous systems.`

Subheadline:

`Calm Studio helps caregivers and practitioners run short, predictable sessions designed to reduce sensory surprise.`

Secondary CTA:

`View safety defaults`

### What Calm Studio Does

- time-boxed sessions
- soft start and soft end
- volume ceilings
- caregiver check-ins
- safer-next-session recommendations

### How It Works

1. Choose a goal.
2. Run a short session.
3. Log what happened.

### Safety Promise

Headline:

`We treat overload as a safety event, not a retention problem.`

Body:

`If a pattern is repeatedly associated with distress, Calm Studio should suppress or step away from it.`

### Mini FAQ

Q:

`Is this a cure or treatment?`

A:

`No. It is a caregiver-guided listening environment.`

Q:

`Can I stop instantly?`

A:

`Yes. Stop and quiet fade-out controls should remain visible during every session.`

Q:

`Do you collect child audio?`

A:

`Not by default. Calm Studio can operate using caregiver check-ins and session metadata alone.`

## Safety And Evidence Page

### Hero

Headline:

`No cures. No mysticism. Just careful design.`

Body:

`NOIZYVOX is built on a simple rule: do not promise what you cannot prove, and do not ship listening or voice systems that can harm sensitive users.`

### What We Claim

Use language like:

- predictability
- reduced sensory surprise
- caregiver-guided listening
- creator-governed voices
- consent-first licensing

Do not use language like:

- cure
- treatment outcome guarantee
- diagnostic inference

### Evidence Position

Body:

`The audio-regulation field contains both useful insights and a history of overclaiming. NOIZYVOX takes a research-informed, non-clinical posture and labels evidence strength explicitly.`

### Evidence Ladder

- Exploratory
- Research-informed
- Clinically evaluated
- Clinically validated
