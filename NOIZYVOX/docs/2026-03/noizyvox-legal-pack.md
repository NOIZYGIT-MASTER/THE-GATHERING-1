## Terms Of Service

### Hero

Title:

`Terms of Service`

Subtitle:

`By using NOIZYVOX, you agree to these safety-first, consent-driven rules.`

Microcopy:

`Calm Studio and Regulation Voices are non-clinical services provided as-is.`

### 1. Use Of Platform

- NOIZYVOX provides caregiver-guided listening environments and artist-consented voice profiles.
- Users may not present the platform as medical diagnosis, treatment, or cure infrastructure.
- Safety defaults and consent constraints must not be bypassed.

### 2. Caregiver Responsibilities

- Caregivers are responsible for supervising children during Calm Studio use.
- Overload events must be treated as safety incidents.
- Exports shared with schools or clinicians require permission from the relevant caregiver or guardian.

### 3. Creator Responsibilities

- Creators must truthfully declare allowed and disallowed contexts.
- Revocation requests affect future generation but cannot automatically retract previously exported material.
- Misrepresentation of a voice profile or its permitted use is prohibited.

### 4. Account And Security

- Users are responsible for protecting account credentials.
- Unauthorized access, attempts to bypass consent rules, and attempts to exploit privileged controls are prohibited.
- Privileged internal actions should remain authenticated and allow-listed.

### 5. Content Ownership And Licensing

- Creators retain ownership of their voices and associated identity rights.
- Buyers receive context-limited rights according to the selected license tier.
- Usage receipts may be displayed for accountability, but listener identities are not exposed by default.

### 6. Safety And Governance

- Safety review actions, pauses, and advisory decisions are enforceable under the platform rules.
- NOIZYVOX may temporarily pause content, profiles, or scenes during review.
- Governance decisions should remain versioned and logged.

### 7. Limitation Of Liability

- NOIZYVOX does not guarantee medical, therapeutic, or educational outcomes.
- Use of the platform remains at the user's discretion.
- Research participation is voluntary and anonymized by default.

### 8. Termination

- Accounts may be suspended or terminated for violating safety, consent, or license rules.
- Termination does not transfer creator ownership away from the source creator.

## Privacy Policy

### Hero

Title:

`Privacy Policy`

Subtitle:

`We treat child-first data with restraint and care.`

### 1. Data We Collect

- caregiver check-in outcomes
- trigger tags
- template usage metadata
- account and billing information

### 2. Data We Do Not Collect By Default

- raw child audio
- personal identifiers linked to child listening records
- unconsented behavioral inference

### 3. How We Use Data

- improve safer defaults and recommendations
- support governance and review workflows
- generate anonymized research summaries for opted-in participants

### 4. Sharing And Research

- research participation is voluntary
- raw audio is not shared by default
- aggregated insights may be published
- participants may withdraw opt-in consent

### 5. Security Measures

- privileged operations require authentication
- wildcard CORS should not be used on sensitive actions
- arbitrary execution endpoints should not be exposed
- internal infrastructure details should remain private
- monitoring and allow-listed tunnels should protect sensitive services

### 6. Cookies And Analytics

- essential cookies support reliability and security
- optional analytics can be disabled

### 7. User Rights

- export data
- delete Calm Studio history
- withdraw from research participation

## Transparency Page

### Hero

Title:

`Transparency and Accountability`

Subtitle:

`We show what we do, how we do it, and why.`

### 1. Community Governance

- advisory circles guide safety, voice integrity, and public language
- decision logs track what changed and why
- overload reports are treated as safety events

### 2. Research And Evidence

- anonymized outcomes may inform safer defaults
- evidence labels should distinguish exploratory from clinically evaluated work
- pseudoscientific frequency claims are excluded

### 3. Licensing And Voice Use

- allowed and disallowed contexts are enforced at generation time
- revocation actions are logged
- usage receipts track compliance without exposing private user identities

### 4. Security Posture

- authentication-first controls
- restricted origins for privileged operations
- monitoring, alerts, and audit trails

### 5. Audit And Reporting

- public-facing trust metrics may include tolerance rate, overload rate, and repeat-safe templates
- caregiver and clinician feedback should be incorporated without exposing private child data

## Legal Tone Rules

- use child-first and creator-first language
- stay non-clinical unless a separate validated program exists
- avoid cure, diagnosis, or treatment claims
- keep governance and security language concrete, not decorative

## Success Condition

The legal pack is working when the public surface is clear enough for caregivers, creators, buyers, and reviewers to understand the boundaries of the system without needing hidden interpretation.
