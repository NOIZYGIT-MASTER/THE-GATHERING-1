The Covenant

In the twenty-sixth year of the new century, the human voice became the most valuable territory on earth. The machines had learned to wear it.

The old laws were not enough. Terms of service could be deleted. Contracts could be quietly rewritten. Consent, reduced to a checkbox, dissolved the moment no one was watching.

So a covenant was written — not in policy, but in code. A system that refuses to synthesize a voice without a signed verdict from its owner. A ledger that remembers every decision, forever. A refusal engine that speaks in human words when the answer must be no.

The infrastructure IS the ethics.

This is the threshold.
