CHAOS ARENA

Break the proof if you can.

Scope

This challenge targets NOIZY's consent verification and proof validation layer.

In Scope

  • Consent receipt verification bypass
  • Proof validation circumvention
  • Revocation check failure
  • Bundle integrity compromise
  • Manifest hash collision
  • Timestamp manipulation

Out of Scope

  • Cloudflare infrastructure attacks
  • DDoS or availability attacks
  • Social engineering
  • Physical access attacks
  • Attacks requiring internal system access

Bounty Rewards

Severity Category Reward
Critical Consent verification bypass $5,000
High Proof validation failure $2,500
Medium Revocation check bypass $1,000
Low Bundle integrity issue $500

How to Participate

  1. Use the public verifier at /chaos-arena/
  2. Attempt to break consent verification or proof validation
  3. Document your findings with steps to reproduce
  4. Email security@noizy.ai with:
    • Description of the vulnerability
    • Steps to reproduce
    • Impact assessment
    • Proof of concept (optional)

Technical Context

What NOIZY claims:

  • C2PA provides the provenance primitive (cryptographic signatures, manifest validation)
  • NOIZY provides the governance layer (consent interpretation, revocation semantics)
  • Append-only audit is NOIZY application design
  • Chain integrity is hash-linked and verified on read

What NOIZY does NOT claim:

  • Cloudflare Worker versioning tracks D1/KV state changes (it does not)
  • Platform primitives alone guarantee immutability (they do not)
  • The system is theoretically unbreakable (we want you to try)

Disclosure Policy

  • We will acknowledge receipt within 48 hours
  • We will provide an initial assessment within 7 days
  • We will work with you to understand and fix the issue
  • Public disclosure: 90 days after fix, or coordinated earlier
  • Credit will be given unless you prefer anonymity

Legal Safe Harbor

Security research conducted in good faith under these rules will not result in legal action. We consider security research conducted consistent with this policy to be authorized, lawful, and helpful. We will not pursue civil or criminal action against researchers who discover and report vulnerabilities in accordance with this policy.
