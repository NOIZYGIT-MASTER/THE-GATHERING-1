EPOCH I · PUBLISHING ERA
The Printing Revolution — Sheet Music as Product
Product: Notation (Sheet Music) · Value: Physical Sale / Printing Monopoly
Ottaviano Petrucci secured the first rights to print music in 1498. Power resided in whoever could control the physical reproduction of notation. The music existed only as instructions — the performance was the act. Composers were the content; printers were the infrastructure.
1920s–
1990s
EPOCH II · RECORDING ERA
The Record Label Age — Performance Captured
Product: Performance (Vinyl/Cassette/CD) · Value: Physical Sale of Object
The recording became the product. Labels gained power by controlling studio infrastructure and distribution channels. Artists signed away rights for access to the machine. The recording was permanent, ownable, sellable — but the creator owned almost none of it.
2000s
EPOCH III · DIGITAL DISRUPTION
The File Era — Everything Became Free
Product: File (MP3) · Value: 99¢ Download or Zero (Piracy)
The infrastructure collapsed. Napster and then iTunes destroyed the physical object. The music became weightless. Labels flailed. Artists got pirated. The product was now a file — infinitely copyable, infinitely distributed. The power temporarily fell to no one. Then the platforms noticed.
2010s–
present
EPOCH IV · STREAMING AGE
IP as Financial Asset — Access Beats Ownership
Product: Access (Rights/Streams) · Value: Micro-Payment Licensing
Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube: access replaced ownership. IP became a financial instrument — catalog financing, royalty securitization, streaming fractions. $0.003 per stream. AI companies then began training on streamed content without consent. The extraction intensified. The crisis is now.
