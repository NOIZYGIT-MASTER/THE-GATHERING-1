I · THE ORIGIN STORY

One Room. One Injury. One Refusal.

"The story begins not with a startup, not with funding, not with a product-market fit slide. It begins with a man who sustained a C3 spinal injury and decided that this was not the end of his creative life. It was the beginning of a new architecture for it."

Robert Stephen Plowman is a composer and audio director. Ottawa-born. Decades in the music and audio industry — film, television, interactive media, the full arc. By the time he founded NOIZYFISH INC. in early 2026, he had seen the industry from the inside across four full epochs: publishing, recording, digital, and streaming.

The C3 spinal injury changed what was possible. It did not change what was necessary. What was necessary was a way to keep creating — at full power, at full scale, at full depth — from exactly where he was. What he built in response to that necessity became the foundation of NOIZY.

He called it GORUNFREE. 35% voice. 65% AI. 1-click. Zero friction. He was careful to say that GORUNFREE was not a productivity philosophy. It was a survival architecture. The distinction matters — because survival architectures are designed to be absolutely reliable, absolutely accessible, and absolutely non-negotiable. You cannot have a bad day with a survival architecture. It has to work every time.

That same design philosophy — build it so it cannot fail, build it so it cannot be extracted from, build it so that every human who needs it can reach it — became the DNA of every system in the NOIZY ecosystem. GORUNFREE was not the product. GORUNFREE was the blueprint.

"He did not wait for the world to accommodate him. He built infrastructure that made the world accommodate everyone. The C3 injury was not an obstacle to NOIZY. It was its origin story."

VIII · THE MOVEMENT

Counteracting the Takeover

"Rob did not describe what he was building as a product. He described it as a counteraction — a direct, structural response to the takeover of the arts by greed and exploitation."

The platforms of 2026 had built their empires on a simple thesis: creators are inputs, not stakeholders. Their voices, their compositions, their likenesses, their creative labor — all of it was raw material to be processed into platform value. The creator received a fraction of a fraction, denominated in a currency (the stream) that was designed to make the fraction feel larger than it was.

NOIZY's counteraction was not a protest. It was not a manifesto nailed to a platform's door. It was infrastructure. Five architectural principles — Consent-as-Code, the 75% Hardcoded Royalty, the Voice Estate, NOIZY PROOF Watermarking, and the 500-Year Codex — each one designed to make the extraction model structurally impossible, not merely unethical.

The Creator Bill of Rights for AI gave the movement its public face: five rights, each enforced by specific Never Clauses, each explained in language that any creator could understand. Consent. Credit. Revenue Share. Transparency. Portability. Not aspirations. Code.

The Race to the Bottom — the structural problem that VC-subsidized underpricing and algorithmic volume incentives created — was diagnosed not as a moral failure but as an architecture failure. You cannot ethics your way out of a structural problem. You can only build infrastructure that makes the old architecture obsolete.

"We didn't change the extractive companies. We made extraction unacceptable infrastructure. That's the difference between advocacy and architecture."
