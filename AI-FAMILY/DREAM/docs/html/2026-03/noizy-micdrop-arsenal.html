01 THE RACE TO THE BOTTOM — UNDERSTAND IT COLD

Why This Is Happening

The race to the bottom in creator economics isn't a morality problem. It's a structural problem. Platforms are designed to reward speed and scale — not artistic value, not human creativity, not fairness. AI accelerates this by making cheap content infinitely cheaper. The result is systemic — and the only solution is new infrastructure.

01

Misaligned Market Incentives

Platforms profit on volume and engagement, not creator earnings. More content = more ad inventory = more revenue for the platform. Your payout is a line item to minimize. The incentive structure is architecturally opposed to creator prosperity.

02

Normalized Free & Cheap Content

Streaming trained billions of people to expect music at near-zero cost. AI tools trained a generation to expect professional-quality content for $10/month. The floor keeps dropping because audiences have been conditioned by platforms with unlimited VC subsidy.

03

Structural Pressure, Not Ethics Failure

Individual creators aren't failing morally. They're responding rationally to a system that punishes premium pricing and rewards volume. You can't ethics your way out of a structural problem. The solution must be at the infrastructure level.

04

AI Amplifies the Existing Problem

AI doesn't create the race to the bottom — it accelerates it by removing friction from cheap content production. Without consent infrastructure, AI also strips creators of ownership of the very thing that made the training data valuable: their voice.
