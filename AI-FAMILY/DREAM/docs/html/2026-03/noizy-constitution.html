The NOIZY
Constitution

Human Voice Sovereignty. Five Immutable Laws. The founding law document that encodes the soul of NOIZY as enforceable structure — not marketing language, not promises, not terms of service. Law.

We, the founders of NOIZYFISH INC., declare that the human voice is the most intimate form of identity a person possesses. It carries their biology, their history, their emotion, and their will. In the age of artificial intelligence, the voice must not become a resource to be mined without consent, synthesized without authorization, or commercialized without compensation. This Constitution establishes the non-negotiable principles by which NOIZYVOX operates. These principles are not aspirations. They are architecture. They are law. Break any one of them and NOIZY becomes just another tool.
ARTICLE I
THE FIVE IMMUTABLE LAWS

What Cannot Be Changed

These five laws are non-negotiable. They cannot be removed by investor pressure, by enterprise demands, by regulatory convenience, or by the desire to grow faster. They are encoded in the system architecture, enforced at the pipeline layer, and constitutionally protected here. Any proposed change to these laws requires a constitutional amendment process — formal, recorded, and subject to RSP_001 veto.

I
Consent Is Executable
Consent is not a checkbox. Not a terms-of-service acceptance. Not a one-time signature. Consent is a live cryptographic token, issued by the consent-service, signed by HSM, verified at every synthesis request before any audio is generated. If the consent token is not present, active, and valid — no synthesis occurs. There are no exceptions.
ENFORCED AT: consent-service → 7-stage pipeline stage 1 → synth.requested event requires active token → provenance.signed embeds consent key hash
II
Revocation Is Sacred
If a human being cannot stop their voice instantly, sovereignty is a fiction. Revocation is not a support ticket. Not a 72-hour processing window. Not subject to ongoing license obligations. The moment an Actor invokes withdrawal of consent, all new synthesis stops within five seconds globally. Nothing that has already been completed changes — but nothing new is permitted. "Your voice is resting." Full stop.
ENFORCED AT: POST /provenance/revoke → consent token invalidated → downstream jobs cancelled → Ledger: consent.revoked → KPI S-1 target < 5s p99
III
Provenance Is Default
Every output from NOIZYVOX is born with cryptographic provenance. A C2PA-compatible signed manifest is generated and attached to every synthesis output before it leaves the system. There is no option to generate output without provenance. There is no "legacy mode" without provenance. No provenance equals no output. The manifest travels with the file — not with the platform. If NOIZY shuts down tomorrow, every file it ever produced remains verifiable by any C2PA-compatible tool.
ENFORCED AT: provenance-service → provenance.signed event required before output delivery → KPI T-1 target: 100% provenance coverage
IV
The Human Is the Platform
The Actor is not a user. Not a content creator. Not a vendor. The Actor is the platform. Their voice is the product. Their consent is the authorization. Their royalty floor (85% for Founding RSPs) cannot be renegotiated downward by any business agreement, API integration, or enterprise contract. NOIZYVOX is the infrastructure that serves the Actor, not the other way around. The system routes value to the human automatically — they do not chase it, negotiate it, or sell it themselves.
ENFORCED AT: D1 CHECK CONSTRAINT royalty_pct ≥ 75 (85 for Founding RSPs) · lifeluv-contracts layer enforces before payout.sent event
V
Preservation Is Not Optional
A voice is not a file. It is a human life expressed as audio. The dna-archive preserves Voice DNA packages in accordance with OAIS ISO 14721, with PREMIS metadata that records every migration event. The Voice Estate extends inheritance rights to designated heirs. When a human body ends, their voice — and the performance archive they built — does not disappear. It is preserved with the same care as a national archive. This is not a feature. It is a founding obligation.
ENFORCED AT: dna-archive → PREMIS migration events → estate.inheritance.routed Ledger event → Voice Estate directive required at enrollment

PLAYGROUND ARTICLE 1 · TWO MODES ONLY
Riff Mode and Write Mode. Nothing Else.
Riff Mode is for improvisation: character discovery, emotional exploration, raw performance growth. No scripts. No scores. No judgment. Commands: "Switch to [Descendant]" / "Next" / "Save that" / "Make it softer / darker / closer" / "That's enough." Write Mode is for construction: dialogue, scenes, monologues, world-building. The system assists — fills secondary characters, maintains continuity, suggests tonal shifts — but never takes authorship. Every feature added to the Playground must belong to one of these two modes or it does not belong.
ARCHITECTURAL RULE: Playground features are Creativity Rail. They may not import from Trust Rail services except via the consent-validation gateway. The system checks consent before every session begins. It does not check again during the session.

ARTICLE IV
THE TRUST SPINE

Where NOIZY Becomes Untouchable

The Trust Spine is the enterprise rail — the set of services that make NOIZYVOX sovereign-by-design rather than sovereign-by-promise. It is always on. It cannot be disabled by feature flags, by API integrations, or by enterprise customization. The Trust Spine is the difference between a platform and infrastructure.

TRUST ARTICLE 1 · CONSENT-AS-CODE
No Contract Can Override This
Every synthesis request requires an active consent token, validated in real time by consent-service. Never Clauses NC_01–NC_10 are immutable — they are not consent terms that can be negotiated away, they are system-level gates that fire before synthesis begins. No enterprise contract can remove a Never Clause. No API integration can bypass consent validation. This is enforced at the architecture layer, not the policy layer.
ENFORCEMENT: consent-service is a hard dependency of voice-engine. voice-engine cannot be called without a valid consent-service response. This is an architectural constraint, not a runtime check.
TRUST ARTICLE 2 · PROVENANCE BY DEFAULT
No Provenance = No Output
Every generated artifact is born with a C2PA-compatible signed manifest. The manifest contains: Actor identity hash, consent key hash, Never Clause clearance, watermark (WM_SHA256), synthesis timestamp, and royalty floor. The manifest is embedded in or associated with the audio file. It travels with the file. A 2126 archivist can verify provenance without contacting NOIZYVOX, without trusting NOIZYVOX, and without NOIZYVOX existing.
ENFORCEMENT: provenance-service.issueProvenance() must return SUCCESS before output is delivered. Output delivery is gated on provenance.signed Ledger event.
TRUST ARTICLE 3 · AUDIT WITHOUT SURVEILLANCE
The System Answers Questions. It Does Not Watch the Creator.
The NOIZY Ledger answers: who authorized this, under what terms, when, was it allowed. It does not record how long an Actor spent in the Playground. It does not track engagement time, session frequency, or creative output velocity. The audit exists for compliance, royalty, and provenance purposes — not to monitor creator behavior. The creator is never the subject of surveillance. They are the source of authority.
TRUST ARTICLE 4 · VOICE ESTATE CONTINUITY
Voices Do Not Disappear When People Do
At enrollment, every Actor defines a Voice Estate directive: who controls the voice after their death, how earnings flow to heirs, what uses are forever denied post-mortem. The directive is stored as a PREMIS Rights entity in the dna-archive. When the estate.inheritance.routed Ledger event fires, the provenance chain continues under the heir ActorID. The voice — and everything the Actor built — persists with the same sovereignty architecture that protected it in life.
PRESERVATION: dna-archive → PREMIS Rights entity → estate.inheritance.routed event → new ActorID for heir → provenance chain unbroken across mortality
