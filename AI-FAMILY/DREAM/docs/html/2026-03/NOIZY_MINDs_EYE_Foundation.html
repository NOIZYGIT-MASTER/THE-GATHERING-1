The Problem We're Solving

Current Audio Description Is Broken

The technology exists. The will does not. Current AD is flat, clinical, poorly written, robotically delivered, and completely identical for every listener regardless of their preferences, context, or needs. It insults the intelligence and imagination of the people it claims to serve.

"The scene shows a city street at night. Cars drive by. A woman is standing near a building."
Generic. Emotionless. No atmosphere. No context. No personalization. The same for everyone. Worse than nothing because it suggests this is what you deserve.
✅ MIND'S EYE — What We Will Build
"The door opens slowly — you can hear the weight of what's about to happen in the pause before he enters. The room is warm and dim, familiar furniture casting long shadows. He lowers himself into the chair the way a man does when he's been carrying something too long. Across the table, she hasn't looked up yet."
Cinematic. Emotional. Intelligent. Atmospheric. Infused with sonic texture. And learned to your exact preferred level of detail.

What Rob Heard on AMI Radio — The Complaint List That Started Everything

Too generic — AD describes what's visible without conveying what it means. Tone, tension, and subtext are completely absent.
Robotic delivery — Monotone voice reads descriptions with no emotional intelligence. The voice cannot match the mood of the scene.
One size fits all — A cinephile and a casual viewer get identical descriptions. A child and an adult get the same words. No adaptation, ever.
Talks too much or too little — No way to tell the system "I want more atmosphere" or "just give me the essentials." Fixed verbosity forever.
No sonic atmosphere — Pure dry narration. No musical texture underneath. No spatial audio. No sensory immersion whatsoever.
Disrupts the experience — Descriptions feel bolted on, interrupting the emotional flow rather than weaving into it seamlessly.

The NOIZY Musical Palette

Every Scene Gets Its Sonic Truth

MIND'S EYE doesn't just narrate. It scores. Using every musical element inside NOIZY.AI — Rob's music, NOIZYFISH's emotional frequency mapping, and the DreamChamber sonic palette — each scene is painted with the sound it deserves.

Scene Type: Tension / Danger
"The hallway stretches ahead in near darkness. Something has changed in the air — you can hear it before anything happens."
Musical Layer: Low cello drone 40–80 Hz. Slow heartbeat polyrhythm. Minor 2nd harmonic tension. Spatial: sound compressed to center (claustrophobia). Volume: very low beneath narration.
Scene Type: Joy / Celebration
"The room erupts. People moving in every direction, faces open with it — the kind of happiness that fills a space and makes it feel larger than it is."
Musical Layer: 528 Hz bright harmonic. 5:4 playful polyrhythm. Major pentatonic lift. Spatial: wide stereo, full room feel. 100–120 BPM underlying pulse.
Scene Type: Loss / Grief
"She doesn't move for a long moment. The room is very still around her — the kind of still that happens when something has just changed permanently."
Musical Layer: 396 Hz Solfeggio. Descending minor phrase, unresolved. 55 BPM. Cello with space between notes. Reverb: long (cathedral). Almost silence with emotional texture.
Scene Type: Love / Intimacy
"They're very close now — closer than they've been before. Something has shifted that neither of them named but both of them felt."
Musical Layer: 639 Hz. Breathy vocal harmonics 100–300 Hz (vagus activation). 65 BPM lullaby rhythm. Binaural: narration spatially near, intimate. Warmth in the bass register.
Scene Type: Wonder / Discovery
"Whatever this is — it wasn't here before. The way the light is catching it, the way everyone has gone quiet. Something important is happening."
Musical Layer: Rising melodic arc that doesn't resolve. Suspended harmonics. 85–100 BPM. Lydian mode. Gentle reverb expansion. Alpha-range binaural underneath.
Scene Type: Transcendence / Awe
"This is too large to take in all at once. The scale of it — you'd have to stand there a long time before the full weight of it lands."
Musical Layer: 963 Hz + 40 Hz gamma. Vast reverb tail (8+ seconds). Nested fractal polyrhythm. Spatial: full 360°, sounds arriving from all directions. Near silence underneath everything.
