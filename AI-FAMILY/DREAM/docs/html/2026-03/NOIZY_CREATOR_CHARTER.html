The Creator Bill of Rights for the AI Era

THE NOIZY
CREATOR CHARTER

A founding covenant between human creators and artificial intelligence —
written to endure longer than any platform, any model, any era.

We, the creators — singers, composers, producers, poets, voice artists, beatmakers, lyricists, sound designers, musicians of every tongue and tradition — having built the world's most powerful cultural currency, having watched that currency extracted, commodified, and algorithmically replaced without our consent, credit, or share — do hereby establish this Charter as the irreducible floor of our rights in any system, platform, or protocol that touches our work.

This Charter belongs to no company. It belongs to every creator who signs it. It asks AI to be what it was always capable of being: not a replacement, but a collaborator bound by conscience.

Foundation

The Five Pillars

Every article of this Charter is rooted in one or more of these five pillars. No platform may claim to honor creators while violating any of them.

I
Consent
No AI may train on, replicate, or remix your work without explicit, revocable, informed consent.
II
Credit
Every use of your work — direct or derivative — must be traceable back to you, in perpetuity.
III
Revenue Share
Economic value generated by your work flows back to you. Minimums are guaranteed. No exceptions.
IV
Transparency
You have the right to see exactly how your work is used, who used it, and what it earned.
V
Portability
Your rights, your voice, your catalog — portable across any platform, forever yours to move.
The Charter

Ten Articles

These ten articles form the complete, non-negotiable framework of creator rights in the age of AI. They apply to NOIZY, and we invite every platform on earth to adopt them.

01
The Right of Consent Before Training
Pillar I — Consent
  • No AI model, system, or platform may ingest, train on, or extract patterns from a creator's work without prior, explicit, written consent.
  • Consent must be specific to use case — training consent does not imply synthesis consent, and neither implies commercial deployment consent.
  • Consent is revocable at any time. Revocation applies to future use and must be honored within 30 days. Prior licensed uses may continue under their original terms only.
  • Consent cannot be buried in terms of service. It requires a standalone, plain-language acknowledgment.
02
The Right of Perpetual Attribution
Pillar II — Credit
  • Every work carries the creator's name, registered identity, and creation timestamp in an immutable metadata chain — regardless of how many transformations it undergoes.
  • AI-generated works that draw from human source material must disclose the provenance chain to any requesting party.
  • Attribution cannot be waived by contract or obscured by anonymization. Credit is not a courtesy; it is structural.
  • Where a work has multiple contributors, each is attributed proportionally. AI contribution is labeled as such and carries no human credit.
03
The Right to Revenue — The 75/25 Guarantee
Pillar III — Revenue Share
  • When a creator's work generates revenue on NOIZY — through licensing, performance, synthesis, sampling, or AI model deployment — the creator receives a minimum of 75% of that revenue.
  • The platform's operating share is capped at 25%. This ratio is permanent and not subject to renegotiation on a per-creator basis.
  • The 75/25 split activates after one month of enrollment. No probationary periods, no tiered rollouts, no exceptions.
  • Revenue is calculated at source, not after deductions. Creators see the full number before any split is applied.
  • Micro-royalties — payments as small as fractions of a cent — are tracked, accumulated, and paid. No minimum threshold suppresses payment.
04
The Right to an Open Ledger
Pillar IV — Transparency
  • Every creator has real-time access to a dashboard showing: who used their work, when, where, for what purpose, and how much it earned.
  • Algorithmic decisions affecting discovery, promotion, or demotion of a creator's work must be explained in plain language on request.
  • Platforms must publish an annual Creator Economy Report: aggregate revenue flows, average creator earnings by tier, and the percentage of revenue returned to creators versus retained by the platform.
  • No black-box royalty pools. Every dollar must be traceable to a specific use event.
05
The Right to Own Your Voice
Pillars I & II — Consent & Credit
  • A creator's voice — its acoustic qualities, emotional signature, timing, cadence, and style — is biological and legal property. It cannot be cloned, synthesized, or deployed commercially without consent.
  • AI voice models trained on a creator's voice belong to that creator. Platforms may operate them under license; they may not own them.
  • Creators may register their voice signature as an identity record (e.g., NOIZYVOX HVS) and have that registration recognized across participating platforms.
  • If a creator's voice is used in AI training without consent, the creator is entitled to full audit of that use and market-rate compensation for the full duration of that use.
06
The Right to Portability and Exit
Pillar V — Portability
  • A creator may export their complete catalog, royalty history, listener data, voice registrations, and identity records at any time in a standard, machine-readable format.
  • Platforms may not impose exit penalties, cooling-off periods, or data suppression upon a creator's departure.
  • Royalties earned before exit continue to be paid to the creator's designated address for the life of the original license agreements, regardless of platform relationship.
  • Portability of rights is guaranteed. A creator's right to their own work does not terminate when their platform relationship does.
07
The Right to Know When You're Hearing AI
Pillar IV — Transparency
  • All AI-generated audio, music, or voice content distributed through any platform must be labeled as AI-generated at point of delivery to the listener.
  • AI content that incorporates human source material must additionally disclose which human work was used, and that creator must have consented and received compensation.
  • Platforms may not suppress, hide, or de-emphasize AI disclosure labels in exchange for distribution fees or promotional agreements.
  • Listeners have the right to filter by: human-only, AI-only, or human-AI collaborative content.
08
The Right to Mentorship and Knowledge Transfer
Community Pillar
  • Experienced creators have the right and responsibility to mentor emerging creators. Platforms must provide infrastructure that enables this — not just content consumption, but knowledge transmission.
  • Credit flows back through mentorship chains. A Master who trains a Journeyman who trains an Apprentice who creates a hit — every link in that chain is credited and compensated for the part they played in creating the capacity that created the work.
  • Guild knowledge — techniques, tools, craft traditions — is a commons. Platforms may not proprietary-ize community knowledge into locked courses without creator consent and revenue share.
  • Every creator has the right to learn, to grow, and to be seen at every stage of that growth.
09
The Right to Cultural Integrity
Sovereignty Pillar
  • Cultural traditions, oral histories, indigenous music systems, regional dialects, and community-specific sonic languages belong to their originating communities. AI may not commodify them without community-level consent and community-level revenue share.
  • When AI creates works in the stylistic tradition of a living culture, it must acknowledge that tradition, and pathways must exist for revenue to flow back to source communities.
  • Creators from marginalized, under-resourced, or historically extracted communities receive the same 75/25 guarantee as all other creators — and platforms have an affirmative obligation to not replicate pre-existing structural inequities in their algorithmic systems.
  • Music is not neutral data. It carries identity, memory, and belonging. These are not to be optimized away.
10
The Right to a Living Standard — Not Just Survival
Future Pillar
  • The goal is not merely to prevent harm. The goal is to build a creator economy in which a working musician can earn a sustainable living from their craft — without requiring viral scale, label deals, or algorithmic luck.
  • Platforms that generate billions of dollars in annual revenue while the median creator earns less than minimum wage have failed their stated mission, regardless of their terms of service. This Charter rejects that outcome as acceptable.
  • NOIZY commits to publishing creator median income data annually and to treating any failure to improve that figure as a governance and product failure — not just a market condition.
  • AI is powerful enough to flood the world with content. It is also powerful enough to ensure every human creator who builds on this platform can build a life. We choose the second path.

The Covenant Goes Both Ways

Rights & Obligations

A charter that protects creators must also ask something of them. And it must bind the platform to reciprocal obligations.

Platform (NOIZY) Commits To
What We Promise You
  • Honor the 75/25 split in every transaction, forever
  • Never train on your work without explicit consent
  • Publish the full algorithm logic affecting your discoverability
  • Display an annual Creator Economy Report with median creator earnings
  • Guarantee full data portability with no exit penalties
  • Label all AI content clearly and without suppression
  • Maintain the Guild infrastructure for mentorship and knowledge transfer
  • Submit to third-party audit of royalty flows annually
  • Never suppress creator voices in favor of AI-only content distribution
  • Treat the gap between creator earnings and platform revenue as a problem to be solved, not a market condition to be managed
Creators Commit To
What You Promise the Community
  • Register your work with accurate metadata — authorship, date, collaborators
  • Disclose AI tools used in creation so listeners can understand the work
  • Respect the consent rights of other creators whose work you sample or build from
  • Participate in Guild mentorship — to teach what you know to those coming behind you
  • Not misrepresent AI-generated work as entirely human-created
  • Engage constructively with the governance processes that shape platform policy
  • Respect cultural origins — acknowledge what you draw from, and credit those traditions
  • Uphold the standard: when AI serves creators well, say so; when it does not, say that too
