# DreamChamber Imperial Charter — The Five Permanent Laws

These are operational constraints, not aspirational slogans.
Every system decision is tested against them.

---

## Law I — Receipts precede trust

No action is trusted without a receipt.
No payment is valid without a lineage record.
No consent is real without a machine-verifiable signature.

## Law II — Recognition precedes automation

The creator must be identified before any automated action runs on their behalf.
Identity is not assumed. It is established, recorded, and revocable.

## Law III — Visibility precedes power

No system gains authority over creative work until that authority is visible to the creator.
Opacity is disqualifying.

## Law IV — Revocation precedes regret

Every permission granted must have a revocation path.
If a right cannot be taken back, it should never have been granted.

## Law V — Creator signal > all other signals

When any system input conflicts with the creator's expressed intent,
the creator's signal wins.
Always.

---

## Constitutional note

These laws are not features.
They are the filter through which all features are evaluated.
If a feature violates any law, the feature does not ship.
