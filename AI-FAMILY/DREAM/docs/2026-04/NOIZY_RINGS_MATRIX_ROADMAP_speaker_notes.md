# NOIZY — Upgrade Rings · Agent→Brand Matrix · Evolution Roadmap
## Speaker notes, 3–4 minute walkthrough

**Use:** deliver cold to investors, partners, or operators. Three slides, one arc: *speed is deliberate, agents are assigned, the system is designed to grow.*

Pace guide: ~140 wpm. Pause at every em-dash. Slow the final line.

---

### SLIDE 1 — Upgrade Rings (Core / Adaptive / Stable)
**Target time: 60–75 seconds**

**Hook (20 sec, before the click):**
Most ecosystems break for one reason — they treat every product like it should move at the same speed. We do the opposite. NOIZY upgrades are deliberately non-uniform across the empire. This isn't caution. It's design.

**Body (45 sec, on the Rings slide):**
Three rings. Core. Adaptive. Stable.

**Core** is where experimentation lives — NOIZY.AI orchestration, GABRIEL reasoning, the control plane. It moves weekly. Break things here, learn fast, contain the blast radius.

**Adaptive** is the creative surface — LAB, FISH, DREAMCHAMBER. It moves in sprints. Fast enough to chase the culture, slow enough to respect the artists inside it.

**Stable** is the public-facing trust layer — NOIZYKIDZ, NOIZYVOX, anything touching a child, a voice, or a name. It moves on quarterly governance cycles. Stability here isn't conservatism. It's consent infrastructure.

**Transition (5–10 sec):**
Rings tell you how fast each layer moves. The next question is *who* lives inside each layer.

---

### SLIDE 2 — Agent → Brand Matrix
**Target time: 60–75 seconds**

**Body:**
Every brand has an assigned agent. Every agent has a mandate.

**GABRIEL** sits across every column. It's the thinking layer, the provenance layer, the memory layer. Nothing ships without it.

**NOIZYKIDZ** has a dedicated Guardian Agent. Safety by design, audited, non-negotiable. This is where we spend our strictest rules — and we're proud of that.

**LAB and FISH** share the Apple Creative pipeline, because that's where creation actually happens, and that's where creative tooling earns its keep.

The matrix answers the question every investor and partner asks on the first call: *which agents actually improve which products?* You can read that off the wall, today, without a meeting.

**Transition (5–10 sec):**
So — we know the speed. We know the agents. Last question: where is this going.

---

### SLIDE 3 — Evolution Roadmap (Stabilize → Accelerate → Autonomize)
**Target time: 60–75 seconds**

**Body:**
Three phases. Stabilize. Accelerate. Autonomize.

**Stabilize** is now — hardening the rings, locking the consent layer, finishing the provenance chain.

**Accelerate** is next — releasing the Adaptive layer to move at creative speed, with GABRIEL watching everything.

**Autonomize** is the horizon — brands that can grow without being rebuilt. Agents that evolve inside their rings. Products that improve themselves under audit.

We are not building apps that need to be replaced. We are building systems that can grow.

**Closing line (15–20 sec, slow):**
One sentence to take with you:

> *All brand autonomy is bounded by NOIZY.AI orchestration and audited through GABRIEL-driven provenance logs.*

That's the deal. That's the protection. That's the empire.

---

## Delivery notes

- **The hook is earned by the first claim.** Don't soften "We do the opposite." Land it.
- **On Stable:** Say "consent infrastructure" slower than the rest of the sentence. That phrase does a lot of work.
- **On the Matrix:** Resist the urge to read the grid. Name 2–3 intersections, let the eye do the rest.
- **On Autonomize:** The line *"systems that can grow"* is the thesis of the entire company. Don't rush it, don't decorate it.
- **Final line:** The three "That's the…" beats are a rhythmic close. Allow a full second between them. Do not add a word after "empire."

## If you have 2 minutes instead of 4

Keep: hook, the Core/Adaptive/Stable definitions, the GABRIEL line, the Stabilize/Accelerate/Autonomize names, the closing sentence.
Cut: transitions, the LAB/FISH detail, the "read it off the wall" line.

## If you have 6 minutes

Add between slides 2 and 3: one concrete example of a past decision the Rings model would have changed (or did change). Specificity earns the abstraction.
