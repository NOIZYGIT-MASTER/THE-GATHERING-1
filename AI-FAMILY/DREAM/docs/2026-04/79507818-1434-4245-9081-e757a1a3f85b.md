## assistant

To use an iPad as an audio input in Audio Hijack, you have two routes. The iPad 2 (2011, iOS 9 max) is the limiting factor here — it does **not** support most modern options.

**Option 1 — USB (works on iPad 2)**
1. Connect iPad to Mac with the **30-pin → USB cable**.
2. On Mac: open **Audio MIDI Setup** → **Window → Show Audio Devices**.
3. The iPad won't appear as an audio device by default. On modern iPads you'd "Enable" it here, but **iPad 2 / iOS 9 does not expose itself as a USB audio class device** to macOS — Apple only added that with newer iOS versions.
4. Result: ❌ doesn't work on iPad 2.

**Option 2 — Lightning/USB-C iPads only (won't help you)**
Newer iPads show up in Audio MIDI Setup as an input after you tap "Trust" — then you select them in Audio Hijack's Input Device block. iPad 2 can't do this.

**What actually works for an iPad 2:**

- **3.5mm headphone-out → Mac audio interface line-in** — analog, lossy, but reliable. Plug iPad's headphone jack into your interface's line input, then in Audio Hijack add an **Input Device** block set to that interface's input channel.
- **AirPlay receiver app** on the Mac (e.g., **Rogue Amoeba's Airfoil** — same company as Audio Hijack). iPad 2 supports AirPlay. Airfoil Satellite or **Loopback** routes the AirPlay stream into Audio Hijack as a virtual input.
- **Bluetooth A2DP** — pair iPad to Mac, capture via Audio Hijack's Bluetooth input. Quality is mediocre.

**Best path for iPad 2 → Audio Hijack:** Airfoil (AirPlay receive on Mac) → Audio Hijack captures Airfoil's output. Cleanest digital route given the hardware limit.
