# How NOIZY Writes Truth

We don't put everything into permanent records immediately.

Some things need to prove themselves first.

---

## Two Layers of Memory

### Fast Memory

- Used to make the system feel instant
- Lets us adapt and learn safely
- Can be rolled back

### Truth Memory

- Used for accountability and explanation
- Only written after safeguards pass
- Designed to last

---

## What We Require Before Writing Truth

We only record something permanently when:

- It behaves reliably under real use
- It does not harm creators
- It respects consent
- It remains stable over time

If those conditions are not met, we don't write it.

---

## What This Protects You From

- Early assumptions becoming permanent
- Data that cannot be explained later
- Silent changes to creative boundaries
- Hidden replication of restricted material

---

## Why This Matters

It means:

- Fast ideas don't become false facts
- Creativity stays fluid
- Trust stays intact
- Mistakes can be reversed

---

## Our Principle

> **Speed earns attention.
> Proof earns permanence.**

---

## What You Can Always See

- Where your creation came from
- Who consented to what
- What we didn't copy
- How the system reached its conclusions

---

## What We Never Do

- Write speculative data as fact
- Store unverified provenance claims
- Record consent that hasn't been confirmed
- Keep data that can't be explained

---

## The Technical Reality

Our system has two memory layers by design:

**Fast Layer** — responds instantly, adapts quickly, forgets safely

**Truth Layer** — only accepts data that has earned its place

The fast layer lets us serve you immediately.
The truth layer lets us stand behind our work.

---

## Questions?

If you want to understand how any specific piece of data was written, we can explain it.

Every record in our truth layer has a provenance trail that shows:
- When it was written
- What signals allowed the write
- Who or what authorized it
- How it can be verified
