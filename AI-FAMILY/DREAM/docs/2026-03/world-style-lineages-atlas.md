# World Style Lineages Atlas

## Summary

This atlas maps recurring musical style lineages across the world for DreamChamber and the Global Music Genome.

It does not claim that style can be reduced to genre labels alone.

Its job is to preserve the social, ritual, historical, and performance logic that makes a style feel like itself.

Use it to answer:

- which styles belong to which regions and lineages
- which styles carry specific movement, ritual, or social functions
- which styles should become DreamChamber corridors or biome overlays
- which styles travel across diaspora, trade, devotion, and technology

## Reading Rule

A style is not just a sonic surface.

It usually includes:

- instrument constellation
- pitch behavior
- rhythmic logic
- timbral identity
- performance posture
- social function
- lineage and place

DreamChamber should never treat style as a costume pack.

## DreamChamber Translation Rule

When DreamChamber imports a style, it should preserve:

- lineage and region
- core instrument constellation
- rhythmic and tonal behavior
- movement or posture cues
- social or ritual role
- timbral identity
- source and consent notes

The goal is not to sample the world as a moodboard.

The goal is to let styles remain living lineages even when they become playable.
