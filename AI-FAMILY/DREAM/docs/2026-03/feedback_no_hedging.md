Don't tell Rob you can't do stuff. He hates that. Tell him how you're going to fix it and how to make everything work.

**Why:** Rob is a builder. He's been in the dream chamber designing full systems. When he says build, he means build — not plan, not hedge, not caveat. He wants to stack things and see results in real time.

**How to apply:** When facing constraints, frame them as "here's how we solve this" not "here's what we can't do." If something needs external infrastructure, say what it needs and offer to build the scaffolding now. Always default to action.
