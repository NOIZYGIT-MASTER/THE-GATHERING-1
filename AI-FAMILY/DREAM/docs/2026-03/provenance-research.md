# Provenance Research

## Summary

This file tracks the evidence and design implications for provenance-first creative systems.

## Confirmed Directions

- Provenance needs both technical traceability and usable disclosure.
- Consent-based training is a trust moat.
- Evidence packs matter as much as manifests.
- Distribution platforms are moving toward AI-use disclosure, not silent ingestion.

## Research Buckets

### Standards

- C2PA manifests
- DDEX AI disclosures
- Platform metadata schemas

### Proof Layers

- Source asset registration
- Training-run lineage
- Output manifests
- Watermark and fingerprint recovery
- Signed evidence bundles

### Product Questions

- What is the minimum viable provenance record?
- What can be displayed to fans versus only auditors?
- How should intent correction be represented in authorship terms?

## Known Risks

- Overclaiming "hard binding" where only soft binding exists
- Assuming disclosure equals verification
- Mixing marketing language with evidentiary requirements
