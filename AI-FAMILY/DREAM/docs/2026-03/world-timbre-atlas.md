# World Timbre Atlas

## Summary

This atlas maps recurring timbral identities across world cultures for DreamChamber.

It focuses on how sound feels in the ear, body, and space:

- bright or dark
- dry or resonant
- buzzy or pure
- breathy or metallic
- raw or polished
- near or vast

Use it to answer:

- which timbral families recur across regions
- which timbres imply certain materials, rituals, or performance postures
- which timbral fields should shape DreamChamber visual language and spatial behavior

## Reading Rule

Timbre is not decoration.

In many traditions, timbre carries:

- spiritual authority
- emotional intensity
- regional identity
- instrument lineage
- environmental memory

## DreamChamber Translation Rule

When DreamChamber imports a timbral field, it should preserve:

- material identity
- sustain shape
- attack behavior
- spatial weight
- emotional contour
- lineage and source note

The goal is not only to know what note is played.

The goal is to know what the sound feels like in the body and the room.
