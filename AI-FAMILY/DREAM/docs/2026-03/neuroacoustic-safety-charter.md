# NeuroAcoustic Safety Charter

## Summary

This charter defines the non-negotiable guardrails for any NOIZY system that touches nervous-system support, sensitive listeners, children, or research-grade biosignal loops.

## Core Rule

The system must never frame people as broken problems to be fixed by sound.

The system exists to help nervous systems feel safer, more regulated, and more legible, not to erase difference.

## Non-Negotiables

### 1. No Cure Claims

Do not claim diagnosis, treatment, cure, or prevention without the required evidence and regulatory pathway.

### 2. Regulation Support, Not Human Correction

The product may support calm, predictability, focus, and subjective well-being.

It must not imply that human difference should be normalized away.

### 3. Safety First For Child-Oriented Use

Any child-facing branch must include:

- caregiver or authorized adult controls
- explicit stop controls
- time-boxed sessions
- mandatory rest windows
- no autoplay loops
- soft transitions

### 4. No Raw Audio Collection By Default

Especially for children and sensitive users, raw audio capture must be off by default.

If research capture is ever added, it must be separately consented.

### 5. No Silent Pathology Inference

The system must not infer diagnoses or pathology from voice, behavior, or biosignals without the appropriate regulatory and ethical framework.

### 6. Evidence Labels Must Be Visible

Every program should declare its evidence posture:

- exploratory
- research-informed
- clinically evaluated

### 7. Artist Consent Still Applies

Calm or regulation-oriented voices remain creator-governed assets.

Artists can opt out of child-directed or sensitive contexts.

### 8. Community Learning Must Remain Opt-In

Aggregated safety learning may exist, but participation must be voluntary and clearly explained.

### 9. Distress Overrides Completion

Comfort is more important than finishing a session.

The system should treat overload as a safety event, not churn.

### 10. Ethics Review For Structured Studies

If a workflow becomes formal research, it needs:

- informed consent
- study boundaries
- retention rules
- ethics or IRB review where appropriate

## Product Consequences

This charter requires:

- conservative defaults
- human-readable disclosures
- quick exit paths
- audit logs for safety decisions
- transparent moderation and review

## Prohibited Patterns

- mystical frequency promises
- autism-fixing language
- hidden engagement maximization
- dark patterns that extend exposure
- unreviewed child-facing experimentation

## Anchor Line

If a neuroacoustic product cannot protect the most sensitive listener in the room, it is not ready to ship.
