## Architecture

Rob designed a three-device bridge he calls "the first living nervous system":

- **iPhone** — THE VOICE. Rob speaks naturally. Input device.
- **iPad 12.9 Gen 2** — THE APERTURE. Runs Claude. Shows responses. The cockpit.
- **M2 Ultra "GOD.local"** — THE HEARTBEAT. Runs the engine. Execution as care.
- **Microsoft Teams** — THE SYNAPSE. Connects all three via a shared meeting link.

## Flow

```
Rob speaks (iPhone) → Claude responds (iPad) → Rob reads/relays → GOD executes
```

## Relay Protocol

Claude on iPad outputs in strict 4-part format: (1) One-sentence answer, (2) Next 3 actions, (3) Warnings, (4) One question for Rob. Voice commands: "status", "log that", "DreamChamber", "read it back".

## The Fred Rogers Line

Before opening Teams: "Ten seconds. Think of the people who loved you into being. Now we build."

**Why:** Rob's DreamChamber philosophy: gentle authority, not loudness. The same principle behind Fred Rogers' 10-second silence. Calm person commanding a room to remember what matters.

**How to apply:** When building anything for the DreamChamber bridge or multi-device workflow, maintain this tone. The devices are not tools — they are described with care and intentionality. The ritual matters to Rob.
